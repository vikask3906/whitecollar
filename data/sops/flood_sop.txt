NATIONAL DISASTER MANAGEMENT AUTHORITY (NDMA)
STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE — FLOOD EMERGENCY
=================================================

1. PRE-EVENT (Warning Lead Time > 24h)
────────────────────────────────────────
1.1. Activate District EOC upon IMD/CWC flood advisory.
1.2. Alert all gram panchayats in identified flood-prone zones.
1.3. Pre-position relief material: food, water, tarpaulins at district warehouses.
1.4. Pre-deploy boats and rescue teams to high-risk embankments.
1.5. Identify evacuation routes AWAY from rivers; mark on map.
1.6. Open relief shelters: schools, community halls on higher ground.
1.7. Issue public advisory in local language via radio, SMS, loudspeakers.

2. DURING FLOOD EVENT
──────────────────────
2.1. Continuously monitor water levels via CWC gauge stations.
2.2. If water level crosses Danger Level → mandatory evacuation.
2.3. Deploy NDRF/SDRF boats for stranded persons.
2.4. Prioritize rescue: pregnant women, elderly, children, disabled.
2.5. Set up medical camps at each relief shelter.
2.6. Drinking water: deploy RO tankers; distribute chlorine tablets.
2.7. Animal rescue: coordinate with local animal husbandry department.

3. RESOURCE DEPLOYMENT
──────────────────────
3.1. 1 NDRF team per 5 km of flooded river bank.
3.2. Minimum 10 boats (inflatable + motorized) per district.
3.3. 2 relief camps per block with capacity for 500 families each.
3.4. Army columns on standby if flood area exceeds 100 sq km.

4. COMMUNICATION PROTOCOL
──────────────────────────
4.1. EOC to State HQ: updates every 30 minutes during active flooding.
4.2. CWC bulletins relayed to all responders via WhatsApp group.
4.3. Toll-free helpline activated for citizen distress calls.
4.4. Drone survey every 6 hours for situational awareness.

5. POST-FLOOD
──────────────
5.1. Dewatering pumps deployed within 24 hours of water recession.
5.2. Damage assessment teams deployed within 48 hours.
5.3. Temporary housing for displaced families losing permanent shelter.
5.4. Epidemic prevention: fogging, water testing, medical screening.
5.5. Infrastructure repair prioritization: roads > bridges > power lines.
5.6. File detailed flood report to NDMA within 72 hours.
